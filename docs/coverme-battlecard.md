# Queue vs CoverMe

## Core framing

- `CoverMe` is a manager-first class cover and instructor operations product.
- `Queue` should be positioned as the fastest hyperlocal replacement marketplace.

## What CoverMe appears to do well

- Existing traction with club operators and chains.
- Strong manager-facing story around cover operations, compliance, messaging, and reporting.
- Replaces noisy WhatsApp cover groups with a controlled workflow.
- Enterprise-style buying motion is already established.

## What Queue can win on

- `Free to join` for both sides.
- `Pay only on success`: `€4/$4` per completed replacement instead of recurring subscription pressure.
- `Map-first workflow`: studios visible on map, instructors choose service areas visually.
- `Polygon zones + zip/postcode` targeting instead of generic broadcast.
- `Realtime matching` based on subscribed zones.
- `Instructor gets full pay`.
- `Receipts for both sides`.
- `GPS arrival verification`.
- `Stripe KYC` plus document workflow for insurance/compliance.

## Queue wedge

`Find a verified local sub fast, confirm they arrived, and handle payment and receipts in one flow.`

## Where Queue is currently weaker

- No proven operator distribution yet.
- Compliance maturity is likely lower than CoverMe today.
- Insurance/certification verification still needs an operational trust model.
- Timetable and studio software integrations are probably behind.
- Liquidity is not proven in a first city.

## Where Queue should not compete directly

- Full enterprise workforce suite.
- Heavy reporting / HR / payroll narrative.
- Broad “operations platform for clubs” positioning.

## Where Queue should compete directly

- Last-minute replacement speed.
- Local targeting quality.
- Mobile UX.
- Lower adoption friction.
- Payment and proof-of-arrival workflow.

## Positioning line

`Queue helps studios fill last-minute classes with trusted local instructors in minutes, with built-in verification, check-in, payment, and receipts.`

## Risks

- If first-city supply is thin, the app feels dead quickly.
- If verification feels weak, studios will fall back to WhatsApp.
- If the UX is wordy or slow, the map advantage gets wasted.
- If studios and instructors can easily bypass payments, take rate erodes.

## Product focus

- Keep the promise narrow: `find a verified sub fast`.
- Treat everything else as proof:
  - full pay
  - receipts
  - GPS arrival
  - KYC
  - zone targeting
